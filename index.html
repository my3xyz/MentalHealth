Why is mental health important?

Mental health is just as important as your physical health and shouldn't be brushed aside. Mental health problems should be treated just like how you would treat any problem in your body. Mental health problems are real and you can recover from it with the right help and a positive environment.

Your mental health is a priority.
Your happiness is an essential.
Your self-care is a necessity.

TAKE CARE OF YOURSELF

1. Talk to someone

You might get another persective of your problem and venting has the power to calm you down.

2. Medidate & Working out

Meiditation is scientifically proven to improve your physical and mental health. Take a few deep breaths and see how you feel.
Exercise is also proven to improve your health. Even 20 minutes a day can go a long way.

3. Journal

Do a brain dump. Art or write your way through it. Make a gratitude list.

4. Hobbies

Do hobbies like painting, reading, dancing, singing etc to uplift your mood.

5. Affirmations

It's very easy to doubt yourself and get into negative self-talking which can be harmful. Always remember the people who love you, the things you're good at. Take compliments. Appreciate yourself.

6. Counselling

Get psychiatrich help if you feel helpless or confused. They will help you untangle your thoughts and put things into perpective. You will feel better

7. Experience

Watch videos, talk to people, ask about their experience with mental health. Knowing that someone else went through it and recovered can make you feel less lonely and give you hope.

HOW TO HELP SOMEONE WITH A MENTAL ILLNESS

1. Listen

Listening is the most important thing. Try to understand how they without making assumptions

2. Learn

After you've figured out how to they feel, just try to remind them about the good things, educate yourself about the mental illness they have but always remember you are not a doctor. Do ask them to get help if the feeling prolongs for a longer time.
Usually, people with mental illnesses might know what they are suffering from. To identify symptoms, you need knowledge. So, it is important to educate yourselves.

3. Break the stigma

Usually people might feel a sense of shame around mental health issues. Tell them it is okay to feel this way and recobery is possible.
Let them know going to therapy is okay. Geting help is okay.

4. Ask

Ask them how they feel, ask them how you can help, ask them if they need anything, check up on them.
